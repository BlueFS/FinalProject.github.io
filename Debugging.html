Debugging

Quack Quack

When it comes to coding, many things can go wrong. The program might fail to run as expected, might crash after a while, or not even run at all. When reviewing code, the mistakes can be the cause of forgetting a semicolon, missing braces, typos, and more. Likewise, there are many ways to aid in the problem solving, and these solutions range from talking to a rubber duck (also known as rubber-duck debugging) all the way to using AI to read and propose fixes to your code. A method that I sometimes use is an AI that Harvard created to help their students that take CS50. The website name is cs50.ai and it can greatly help you with your coding troubles. It takes on the role of a rubber-duck, but one that can actually talk. Speaking of rubber-ducks, Harvard's computer science students love rubber-ducks, so here's a song about them:
